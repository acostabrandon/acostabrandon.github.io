01 — Capability

CRM Architecture & Lead Digitalization

Problem

Commercial activity lived across inboxes, spreadsheets, and individual reps' notes. Leads arrived in inconsistent formats, pipeline stages meant different things to different people, and leadership had no reliable, current view of what was in play.

Approach

I built a national CRM from scratch — standardizing lead and deal pipelines on one set of stage definitions, replacing ad-hoc intake with digital lead-capture forms that require the fields we need for clean data, and automating territory routing so every new lead lands with the right owner without manual triage.

Result

  • One source of truth for lead and deal status across the commercial team.
  • Required-field intake forms that protect data integrity at the point of entry.
  • Automated territory routing that removes manual hand-offs and delay.
  • Live pipeline visibility for leadership, refreshed from the system of record.

02 — Capability

Client Onboarding Process

Problem

A capital equipment sale isn't finished when the deal closes — it starts a multi-team handoff spanning logistics, installation, clinical training, and certification. Without a defined path, steps slipped between functions and the customer felt the gaps.

Approach

I coordinate each deal cross-functionally from closed order through go-live, sequencing logistics, install, clinical training, and certification into one repeatable flow with clear ownership and status at every stage — supported by standardized SOPs and email templates so nothing waits on an unasked question.

Result

  • A single, predictable onboarding path from closed deal to certified, live site.
  • Clear stage ownership across commercial, logistics, and clinical teams.
  • Reusable SOPs and templates that make each handoff consistent.

03 — Capability

SOP Development & Post-Sale Operations

Problem

Once equipment is in the field, service issues and end-of-life questions don't resolve themselves. Without documented procedures, each case was handled from scratch — slow, inconsistent, and dependent on whoever picked it up.

Approach

I built standard operating procedures for service escalation and for out-of-warranty and end-of-life handling, developed together with our third-party logistics (3PL) warehouse partner so parts, returns, and dispositions follow a defined route rather than an improvised one.

Result

  • Documented escalation tiers that route service cases consistently.
  • Clear out-of-warranty and end-of-life procedures aligned with the 3PL partner.
  • Predictable turnaround and less reliance on individual tribal knowledge.

04 — Capability

RFM Segmentation & Install-Base IntelligenceHighlight

Problem

The picture of who owned what, and how engaged they were, was spread across multiple systems. Without a unified install base, it was hard to tell a dormant account from an active one, or to know which accounts drove the most value — so re-engagement was guesswork.

Approach

I integrated multiple systems into a single install-base model, then applied RFM segmentation — recency, frequency, and monetary value — to score and group accounts. The segments made it obvious where re-engagement effort would pay off and which relationships to protect, and fed directly into a targeted win-back strategy.

Result

  • A consolidated install base drawn from previously disconnected systems.
  • RFM segments that surface dormant, at-risk, and highest-value accounts.
  • A prioritized, RFM-targeted re-engagement and recurring-revenue strategy.
