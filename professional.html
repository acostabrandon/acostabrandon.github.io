Sales Operations Case Studies at EndyMed

EndyMed is a lean medical-device manufacturer, so sales operations is broad by nature. I owned the systems that moved a deal from lead capture to closed-won, shipment, clinical training, and post-sale support. These five projects highlight how I built structure, automation, and operating discipline across the commercial organization.

Project 01

Install-Base Intelligence

Turning scattered systems into one prioritized account strategy.

Problem

EndyMed needed to grow revenue by re-engaging its existing client base — but the data to do it was scattered across systems. That made it hard to see which accounts were high-value, which were dormant, and which were underutilized.

Action

I cleaned and consolidated the install base, joining ERP and device data, Shopify purchase history, and account records into one unified view — then layered RFM and customer-value scoring to prioritize accounts for re-engagement.

Project 04

Operating Standardization

Turning tribal knowledge into auditable, cost-aware procedures.

Problem

As the company grew, post-sale workflows ran on tribal knowledge — service, shipping, training, and replacement requests all needed consistent procedures. One pain point stood out: replacement-tip requests. Customers sometimes reported broken tips caused by improper use, and single tips were shipped as replacements — driving up shipping cost, creating inventory gaps, and limiting oversight.

Action

I developed a library of SOPs across the key operational workflows, each defining the trigger, steps, owner, and systems involved.

Customer Order Management

Signed PQ through ERP entry, shipment, install, training, and onboarding.

Clinical Training Coordination

Prepare customers and trainers for successful, on-schedule installs.

Replacement Tip Request

Handle defective-consumable claims while controlling cost and risk.

CRM Lead-to-Customer

Consistent handling from lead intake to Closed Won and onboarding.

Shipping & Service Approval

Approval thresholds and standards that control logistics cost.

Event Lead Management

Import, dedupe, route, and follow up on post-event leads.

Purchase Agreement / PQ Creation

Standardized, complete quotes ready for approval and signature.

Deep dive — the Replacement Tip Request. Redesigning one workflow shows the operating logic:

Old process

  1. Customer reports a broken tip
  2. A single replacement tip is shipped
  3. Shipping cost incurred each time
  4. Limited oversight of the claim
  5. Repeat-request risk

New process

  1. Customer submits a structured claim
  2. Issue reviewed for eligibility
  3. Shopify store credit issued when appropriate
  4. Customer applies credit to their next box order
  5. Repeat / high-value issues escalated
The business logic: instead of shipping single replacement tips, a client reporting four defective tips could receive roughly a $400 credit toward a future order. Because a full box of tips runs about $1,000, this preserved customer support while improving margin, inventory control, and oversight.
Result
  • Reduced unnecessary shipping and single-tip replacement handling.
  • Created a more controlled, auditable support process.
  • Turned informal service decisions into standardized workflows.
Project 05

PQ Automation

From a 30–60 minute manual quote to seconds, with cleaner data.

Problem

Purchase-agreement (PQ) creation was manual. Reps submitted incomplete information, CRM fields lagged, and discounts, payment terms, warranties, and special requests all had to be reviewed and formatted by hand — 30 to 60 minutes per PQ.

Action

I built a structured PQ request form with required fields that force complete information before a PQ can be created:

Client information Device configuration Handpieces Desired subtotal Payment terms Warranty terms Special requests Approval notes

An Excel / Office Scripts automation then distributes the required discount evenly across line items to hit the desired subtotal — invoicing and order management need the discount allocated per line — and updates warranty, payment terms, and agreement language, ready for approval and client signature.
